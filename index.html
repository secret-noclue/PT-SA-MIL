The Searchlight – An Audio‑Visual Journey

Information Literacy as a journey from confusion to clarity. A symbolic narrative told through images, captions, and voice—moving from fog and noise to confident understanding and responsible sharing.

Storyline

Three symbolic scenes—Fog, Searchlight, Clarity—designed for a slideshow or short video.

Scene 1

The Fog

1

Action: Student sits at a messy desk—papers, phones, and screens show conflicting headlines. Frustration builds.

Symbol: Fog = misinformation and digital noise.

Scene 2

The Searchlight

2

Action: A beam of light appears (flashlight or edit). Student follows it toward the ERC/computer lab.

Symbol: Information literacy guiding the way—finding, evaluating, and using sources.

Scene 3

The Clarity

3

Action: Student collaborates, organizes notes, cites sources, and presents confidently in class.

Symbol: Clear sky, organized bookshelf, glowing screen—knowledge shared responsibly.

Script – Opening (Scene 1: Fog)

“In a world filled with endless headlines and digital noise, it’s easy to feel lost. Confusion surrounds us, and the truth seems hidden in the fog.”

Script – Transition (Scene 2: Searchlight)

“But then, a light appears—a searchlight guiding us toward clarity. This light is information literacy: the skill of finding, evaluating, and using knowledge wisely.”

Script – Development (Scene 3: Learning Clarity)

“With the right tools, we learn to separate fact from fiction. We discover reliable sources, organize our ideas, and collaborate to uncover the truth. Information becomes power when we know how to use it.”

Script – Conclusion (Scene 3 → Presentation)

“From fog to clarity, from confusion to confidence—the journey of information literacy transforms us. And when we share what we’ve learned, we shine the searchlight for others too.”

“Information is power… when we know how to find it.”

Presentation tips & rubric alignment

Keep it clean, relevant, and creative to score high across all criteria.

Tips for success

Clarity first: Each image should advance the story—no filler.
Caption wisely: Short, strong lines that reinforce the theme.
Show skills: Cite sources in a slide or caption; mention evaluation steps.
Polish: Consistent fonts, spacing, and color; smooth transitions.
Delivery: Practice narration; pace with pauses for impact.
